Research Philosophy

My research lies at the intersection of condensed matter theory and computational materials science, with a core focus on the fundamental physics of electron-phonon interactions and polaron quasiparticles.

My current research goal is to implement and apply Tensor Cross Interpolation (TCI) algorithms for evaluating polaron Feynman diagrams in real materials — bridging the gap between model Hamiltonians and first-principles systems. This effort is still at an early stage. In parallel, I build open-source scientific tools to accelerate first-principles workflows in the materials science community.

Current Focus

Research Domains

Domain 01

Polaron Many-Body Theory

Applying Quantics Tensor Cross Interpolation (QTCI) — a tensor decomposition framework pioneered by recent advances in the community — to evaluate Feynman diagrams for multi-orbital polaron systems in correlated electron-phonon models.

Domain 02

Electron-Phonon Coupling

Studying how crystal symmetry influences polaron type and transport anisotropy in transition-metal oxides through first-principles DFT calculations and electron-phonon coupling analysis.

Read More arrow_forward
Domain 03

Scientific Software

Building open-source tools for computational physics, including PolaronTCISolver — a C++ library implementing TCI algorithms for polaron Feynman diagram evaluation (repository currently private, under active development) — and CrystalCanvas, a desktop application for crystal structure modeling and DFT/MD file preparation.
